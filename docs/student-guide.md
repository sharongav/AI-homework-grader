# Student Guide

## Getting Started

1. **Sign in** with the email and password provided by your institution.
2. You'll see your **Dashboard** with enrolled courses and upcoming assignments.

## Submitting Homework

1. Navigate to **Courses** → select your course → **Assignments**.
2. Click on the assignment to see instructions, rubric preview, and due date.
3. Click **Submit** and upload your files (PDF, DOCX, code files, etc.).
4. You'll see a confirmation: "Your work is being reviewed."
5. The remaining attempts counter shows how many resubmissions you have left.

## Viewing Grades and Feedback

Once your instructor releases your grade:

1. Go to **Grades** to see your score.
2. Click on a grade to open the **Feedback Viewer** — a split-pane view showing:
   - **Left pane**: Your submission with highlighted annotations.
   - **Right pane**: Detailed feedback organized by rubric criteria.
3. Annotations are color-coded:
   - 🟢 **Green (Strength)** — things you did well.
   - 🔴 **Red (Deduction)** — areas where points were lost, always with a citation to course material.
   - 🔵 **Blue (Observation)** — general observations and tips.
4. Every deduction links to the specific course material it references — click **Cite:** links to see the relevant lecture content.

## Follow-Up Questions

After a grade is released, you can ask the AI follow-up questions:

1. Click **Ask a Follow-Up** on your feedback page.
2. Ask about specific deductions, request clarification, or ask for study tips.
3. The AI answers based on your course materials — it won't help with other courses or future assignments.
4. If the AI detects a potential grading error, it will flag it for your instructor to review.
5. You have a limited number of follow-up questions per submission (default: 20).

## Requesting an Appeal

If you believe your grade contains an error:

1. Click **Request Appeal** on your feedback page.
2. Write a detailed explanation (minimum 10 characters).
3. Your instructor will review and respond.

## Important Notes

- Grades are produced by AI and always reviewed by your instructor before release.
- You can only see grades that have been approved and released by your instructor.
- Your submission content is treated as private and handled according to FERPA/GDPR regulations.
